6. Reflection

Reflection


This phase taught me how different real user behavior is from my assumptions as a designer. One of the biggest challenges was realizing that what felt “obvious” to me wasn’t always obvious to users. Small interaction details, like how clickable something looks, had a major impact on usability. I also learned the importance of designing around familiar patterns. Participants expected common mobile conventions, and when those expectations weren’t met, they hesitated. Aligning my design with those mental models significantly improved clarity. Most importantly, I learned that iteration is not about large redesigns, it’s about identifying friction points and refining them intentionally. Testing with real users helped me make focused improvements that strengthened the final experience without overcomplicating the interface.
