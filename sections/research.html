1. User Research & Persona Creation

Understanding the Problem


Individuals living with chronic health conditions often manage multiple medications, appointments, and healthcare providers simultaneously. This creates cognitive overload, increases the risk of missed doses, and makes communication with healthcare professionals fragmented. Many existing solutions are either too complex for older adults, lack meaningful healthcare integration, or fail to provide personalized reminders and tracking. Our goal is to design a mobile app that simplifies medication adherence, centralizes appointment management, and enables seamless communication between patients and healthcare providers.The core challenge is balancing simplicity with powerful functionality, ensuring the app is accessible for elderly users while still offering robust tracking and communication features.

User Research Approach


1. Surveys

Distributed online questionnaires targeting individuals with chronic conditions to understand medication routines, reminder habits, and technology comfort levels.

2. User Interviews

Conducted one-on-one interviews to explore emotional challenges, daily struggles, communication gaps with doctors, and existing workaround solutions.

3. Secondary Research

Reviewed healthcare adherence studies, medication compliance statistics, and analyzed competitor apps to identify usability gaps and feature opportunities.

4. Contextual Observation

Observed how users manage prescriptions at home, pill organizers, handwritten notes, alarms, and family involvement.

User Personas

Margaret Atwood, 67

Condition: Type 2 Diabetes & Hypertension

Goals: Never miss medication, simplify doctor visits.

Motivations: Stay independent and avoid hospital visits.

Pain Points: Confusing prescriptions, forgets doses, low tech confidence.

How She Uses the App: Relies on simple reminders, large text UI, and appointment alerts with clear instructions.

Juan Diego, 55

Condition: Asthma & High Cholesterol

Goals: Track long-term trends and communicate efficiently with doctors.

Motivations: Maintain performance at work and active lifestyle.

Pain Points: Busy schedule, forgets follow-ups, fragmented medical records.

How He Uses the App: Syncs appointments to calendar, tracks medication history, sends quick updates to healthcare providers.

Barack Benson, 29

Condition: Rheumatoid Arthritis

Goals: Monitor flare-ups and medication side effects.

Motivations: Improve long-term quality of life.

Pain Points: Complex medication cycles, emotional stress.

How She Uses the App: Logs symptoms daily, tracks patterns, communicates directly with specialist through in-app messaging.
